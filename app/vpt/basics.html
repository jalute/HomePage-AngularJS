Introduction of Video Poker

Video Poker simulates a type of casino game called Jacks or Better Poker and general follows the basic rules of poker. A player deposits money into the Video Poker machine and then bets from one to five credits on the hand. Jacks or Better is when a pair of Jacks or better pays.

The object of Video Poker is to create the best five-card poker hand you can make with two rounds of drawing. Once the cards are dealt, the player selects which cards to keep or hold and the remaining cards are replaced in the second deal. The better poker hand rank you can create, the more credit (or money) you can win. Video Poker is a very popular game because you can win big even with a very small bet (of 25 cents). You can find a Jacks or Better slot machine at most casinos.

History of Video Poker

Video poker is a hybrid of video game and the game of poker. Undeniably video poker is the most recent variant of poker and in the present scenario there are technologically advanced video poker machines which can be seen as a prominent part of many casinos. The image to the right is a poker slot machine that was common in saloons in the 1890s and early 1900s.

In 1980s a strong demand was felt for a poker game where players need not observe the movements and facial expressions of other players but enjoy play poker with ease and comfort. This demand led to invention of video poker and it was in the year 1970 that the first video poker machine walked into casinos. Video poker was ideal way to learn poker by beginners, players only required to check out the payout plans of each machine and adjust their game play accordingly.

The year 1979 marked the launch of Draw Poker and the game took off quickly. This helped in further strengthening the position of video poker and other video slots. At the time gamblers did not trust video slots because they could not see the actual spinning reels. With such a strong demand, video poker machines found a prominent place in Las Vegas casinos. Over the recent years there had been a lot of changes in the game play of video poker owing to the efforts of various companies to make video poker more realistic as the original game of poker. Irrespective of this one fact, it is very evident that with so much popularity, the game of video poker will continue to mesmerize its players year after year.

Full Pay Poker

In Jacks or Better Video Poker, the term "Full Pay" is used to describe a 9/6 payout table as shown above. The 9 refers to the payout for a Full House and the 6 refers to the Flush payout. Pay Tables determine what your payout percentage will be for a perfect play. A 9/6 payout table has a theoretical payout of 99.54% (with max bet). Here are some other payouts:

9/7 = 100.7% <--ideally the best
9/5 = 98.45%
8/6 = 98.39%
8/5 = 97.30%
7/5 = 96.15%

When looking for a machine to play at your casino, pay attention to the payouts and always play max bet to maximize your chances of winning!
